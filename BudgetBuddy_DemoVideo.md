# BudgetBuddy Demo Video Script

## Introduction
1. Start with a title screen showing the BudgetBuddy logo and app name.
2. Say: "This is BudgetBuddy, a lightweight finance app for tracking income, expenses, and savings goals." 

## Step 1: Landing Page
1. Open `index.html` in the browser.
2. Show the landing page and mention the app entry point.
3. Click "Login" or "Register" to continue.

## Step 2: Register a New Account
1. Open `register.html`.
2. Enter a new name, email, and password.
3. Click "Create Account".
4. Mention that user data is stored locally in the browser.

## Step 3: Login
1. Open `login.html` or use the redirected login flow.
2. Enter the registered email and password.
3. Show successful navigation to `dashboard.html`.

## Step 4: Dashboard Overview
1. Point out the dashboard cards: Balance, Income, Expenses, Savings.
2. Mention the Capitec-inspired color palette and clean card layout.
3. Show the responsive navigation links.

## Step 5: Add Transactions
1. Navigate to `transactions.html`.
2. Add income and expense amounts using the form.
3. Show the transaction list updating immediately.
4. Return to the dashboard to show totals and updated chart.

## Step 6: Add a Goal
1. Open `goals.html`.
2. Enter a savings goal name and amount.
3. Show the goal added to the list.

## Step 7: Reports and Wallet
1. Open `reports.html` and explain the monthly income/expense cards.
2. Open `wallet.html` and show the current balance summary.

## Step 8: AI Assistant
1. Open `ai-assistant.html`.
2. Ask a budgeting question like "How can I save more?"
3. Show the assistant response.

## Step 9: Export PDF
1. Open `dashboard.html` or `reports.html`.
2. Click the export button.
3. Mention that the app can generate a transaction PDF report.

## Step 10: Offline and PWA Support
1. Explain that the project includes `manifest.json` and `service-worker.js`.
2. Mention the app can be installed or cached for offline use.

## Closing
1. Summarize the value of BudgetBuddy: simple, local, user-friendly budgeting.
2. Invite the viewer to open `index.html` and try the app.
3. End with: "BudgetBuddy is ready for submission and real-world use."
